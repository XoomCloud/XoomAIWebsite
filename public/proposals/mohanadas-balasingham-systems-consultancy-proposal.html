Outcome

A clear path from acquisition to connected retail operations

The immediate risk is buying technology in isolation. This engagement determines what should stay, what should change, which platforms can exchange data, and where custom development is genuinely required.

Current state

System and access review

Confirm the current Independent Solutions POS, ERP platform, product catalogue, stock process, supplier records, payments, delivery process and available APIs.

Target state

Integration architecture

Map how POS, ERP, ecommerce, customer data, online payments, inventory, suppliers and dispatch should exchange information without duplicate entry.

Decision support

Vendor and build roadmap

Recommend which systems to retain or replace, shortlist suitable vendors, identify custom components, sequence implementation and document budget ranges and dependencies.

Deliverables

What the consultancy produces

Systems blueprint

  • Current-state system and workflow map
  • Target integration architecture
  • Data ownership and synchronisation rules
  • API and vendor dependency register
  • Security, privacy and payment-risk considerations

Implementation roadmap

  • POS and ERP recommendation, including specialist POS options
  • Ecommerce and customer-data approach
  • Inventory, supplier and replenishment workflow design
  • Delivery and exception-management design
  • Prioritised phases, indicative timing and budget ranges
The POS should remain with a specialist retail provider. The consultancy will assess providers such as IdealPOS and SwiftPOS alongside the existing environment; XoomAI will focus on architecture, integration and automation.
