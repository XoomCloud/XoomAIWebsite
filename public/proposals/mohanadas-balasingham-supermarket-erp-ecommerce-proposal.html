Proposal 3 · Custom ERP and ecommerce

One source of truth for stock and sales.

A custom supermarket operations platform connecting product data, inventory, suppliers, online ordering and delivery—with the specialist POS retained as the regulated transaction layer.

Outcome

Keep the shop floor and online store aligned

The platform is designed so a sale through the POS or ecommerce store updates the same inventory position, reducing overselling, duplicated data and manual reconciliation.

ERP core

Products, stock and suppliers

Maintain the product catalogue, stock levels, supplier records, reorder settings, purchase activity and operational reporting in one controlled system.

Ecommerce

Online catalogue and ordering

Give customers a responsive online store with product search, cart, checkout, payment handoff, order confirmation and account history.

Operations

POS and delivery integration

Connect the specialist POS through its approved API, synchronise stock and route online orders into picking, delivery and exception-management workflows.

Proposed scope

Core capabilities for the first release

Back-office ERP

  • Product catalogue and category management
  • Stock-on-hand and adjustment controls
  • Supplier records, pricing and reorder settings
  • Purchase-order preparation and receiving
  • Customer and order records
  • Role-based access and audit history

Ecommerce and fulfilment

  • Online product catalogue, search and cart
  • Checkout and approved payment-gateway integration
  • Real-time or scheduled stock synchronisation
  • Picking, packing and delivery status workflow
  • Customer notifications for orders and delays
  • Documented API layer for approved integrations

Delivery plan

Design, build, validate, launch

1

Discovery and technical confirmation

Confirm the acquired store’s ERP, POS API, SKU volume, product data quality, payment gateway, delivery rules, user roles and migration requirements.

2

Architecture and interface design

Finalise data models, integration rules, user journeys, approval points, security controls and the release-one acceptance criteria.

3

Build and integration

Develop the ERP core and ecommerce experience, connect approved vendors and prepare the required data migration and testing environment.

4

Testing, training and launch

Validate stock and order flows, complete user acceptance testing, train the team, launch the approved release and monitor early production activity.
