02 / Sales process

Lead to onboard

The sales job is to identify operational pain, qualify fit, build confidence and move the prospect into a proper technical discovery. Do not try to design the full solution on the first call.

Lead captured

Source can be website, referral, LinkedIn, partner, event, campaign, quiz or inbound email. Record source, company, contact, industry, team size, current systems and stated pain.

Exit criteria: lead logged, owner assigned, first response sent within one business day.

Initial qualification

Confirm they have repetitive work worth automating, a real business process, decision-maker access and enough urgency to justify change.

Exit criteria: qualified, nurture, partner referral or no fit.

Introductory conversation

Keep it business-led. Discuss wasted time, missed enquiries, manual follow-up, poor visibility, customer response delays and staff capacity. Avoid deep architecture.

Exit criteria: one or two likely workflows identified and value acknowledged by the prospect.

AI Workflow Audit booked

Book 30-45 minutes minimum with the right stakeholders. Sales should brief the technical lead before the call.

Exit criteria: calendar invite sent, CRM updated, pre-discovery notes complete.

Technical discovery

Technical team maps systems, permissions, workflow detail, data sources, risks, integrations and implementation scope.

Exit criteria: recommended starting build, dependencies, pricing path and next steps agreed.

Proposal and close

Proposal should focus on business outcomes, starting workflows, inclusions, setup, managed service, security posture, rollout plan and commercial terms.

Exit criteria: signed agreement, deposit or first invoice, onboarding scheduled.

Onboarding and activation

Client provides access, documents, process knowledge and approval rules. XoomAI configures, tests, trains users and launches with monitoring.

Exit criteria: agent live, team trained, success metrics being tracked.

03 / Qualification

Who is a good fit?

XoomAgent™ is strongest where a business has repeated work, high enquiry volume, scattered systems or staff losing time to low-value admin.

Strong fit signals

  • They receive repetitive emails, enquiries, documents, forms or support requests.
  • Staff copy data between inboxes, spreadsheets, CRMs and line-of-business systems.
  • Leads or customer enquiries arrive after hours and go cold.
  • They have a CRM, Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, booking system, e-commerce platform or document store.
  • They can identify a measurable bottleneck: hours lost, slow response, missed revenue, errors or poor visibility.
  • They want a managed service, not another tool to figure out.

Poor fit signals

  • They only want a novelty chatbot with no business process behind it.
  • No clear workflow, data source, owner or success measure exists.
  • They expect perfect autonomous decision-making with no approval points.
  • They are unwilling to provide access, process knowledge or stakeholder time.
  • Budget is materially below a managed service starting point.
  • They want a one-off experiment but no operational ownership.

04 / Talk tracks

How to explain it without jargon

Use plain commercial language. The goal is not to impress them with AI. The goal is to make them realise the work they tolerate every day can be removed.

30-second opener

XoomAgent™ is a managed AI employee for Australian businesses. It handles repetitive work like inbox triage, lead qualification, customer enquiries, document processing, CRM updates and reporting. We scope it around your actual workflows, connect it to the systems you already use, and manage it for you.

When they ask, “Is this a chatbot?”

A chatbot only talks. XoomAgent™ can take action. It can read, sort, draft, route, update records, prepare documents, follow up leads and escalate to the right person. The chat interface is only one doorway into the work.

When they ask about security

Security is one of the reasons XoomAgent™ exists as a managed service. It runs on dedicated infrastructure, your data is not used to train models, access is permissioned, and actions are auditable. XoomAI is backed by XoomCloud’s managed IT and cyber-security background.

When they ask about price early

Managed deployments start from $2,399 per month, with scope confirmed after the audit. The better way to think about it is: which repetitive role, workflow or lost revenue stream do we remove first?

Do say

  • AI employee
  • Managed AI workforce
  • Repetitive work removed
  • Dedicated infrastructure
  • No model training on your data
  • Human approval where it matters

Do not lead with

  • Agents, orchestration, prompts or vector databases
  • “It can do anything”
  • Fully autonomous decision-making
  • Replacing staff
  • Guaranteed savings without discovery
  • Deep integration promises before technical review

06 / First-call questions

Ask for the workflow, not the technology

The best first calls make the prospect describe the work they hate, the delays they tolerate and the systems they already live in.

Pain

  • What repetitive work is stealing the most time each week?
  • Where do enquiries, tasks or documents get stuck?
  • What does your team complain about doing manually?
  • What do you wish happened automatically before the day starts?

Volume

  • How many enquiries, emails, documents or tickets come through each week?
  • How long does the current process usually take?
  • How often does work happen after hours?
  • How many people touch the same task before it is complete?

Impact

  • What happens when this work is delayed?
  • Does it cost revenue, customer experience, compliance risk or team morale?
  • If this was handled automatically, what would your team do with the time?
  • What would make this worth fixing in the next 30-60 days?

Systems

  • Which inboxes, CRMs, document stores, forms and systems are involved?
  • Where does the data start and where should it end up?
  • What tools already have good information and what is messy?
  • Who controls system access?

Governance

  • Which actions can be automated safely?
  • Which actions need approval before sending or updating?
  • What should always be escalated to a person?
  • Are there compliance or privacy requirements we need to know early?

Decision

  • Who needs to be involved in a workflow audit?
  • What budget range is realistic for a managed service?
  • What timeline matters?
  • What would stop this from moving ahead?

07 / Objection handling

Common concerns and calm answers

Stay direct. Do not over-defend AI. Acknowledge the concern, connect it to XoomAI’s managed model, then move back to business value.

Objection Response Then ask
“We tried AI and it was underwhelming.” Most businesses try a tool, not a workflow. XoomAgent™ is built around operational tasks, connected systems and managed delivery. What did you want that AI tool to actually do?
“We are worried about data security.” Reasonable. XoomAgent™ is designed for permissioned access, audit trails, dedicated infrastructure and no model training on your data. Which data or systems are most sensitive in your environment?
“Will this replace staff?” The stronger use case is capacity, not replacement. It removes repetitive work so staff spend more time with customers and higher-value tasks. Where is your team currently spending time below their skill level?
“Our systems are messy.” That is common. The audit helps work out which workflow is clean enough to start with and what needs tidying before automation. Which process is painful but still consistent enough to map?
“We do not know where to start.” That is exactly what the AI Workflow Audit is for. We map the highest-impact workflows and recommend a sensible first build. Where do you lose the most time each week?
“It sounds expensive.” It is a managed digital employee, not a subscription widget. The value case usually comes from time returned, faster response and fewer missed opportunities. What does the current manual process cost you in hours, delays or missed revenue?

08 / Technical discovery handoff

What sales must capture before handoff

A good handoff stops the discovery call from starting cold. Sales should provide the technical team with enough context to probe intelligently.

Minimum handoff notes

  • Company, contact, role, industry and lead source.
  • Main pain points in the prospect’s own words.
  • Likely first workflow and why it matters.
  • Current systems involved: email, CRM, documents, forms, finance, support, booking or e-commerce.
  • Expected volume: emails, enquiries, documents, tickets, leads or tasks.
  • Any security, privacy, compliance or approval concerns raised.
  • Decision-makers and stakeholders who should attend.
  • Budget signal and desired timeline.

Book discovery when

  • The prospect has at least one clear repetitive workflow.
  • There is a measurable pain or business outcome.
  • They have access to the people who understand the workflow.
  • They are open to a managed monthly service.
  • They understand technical feasibility comes after discovery.
  • They can commit 30-45 minutes with relevant stakeholders.

09 / Onboarding expectation

What happens after the sale

Sales should set expectations early: XoomAI manages the build, but the client still needs to provide process knowledge, access and approvals.

1

Kickoff

Confirm outcomes, stakeholders, first workflows, systems, success metrics, communication channel and rollout timeline.

2

Access and knowledge

Collect required documents, SOPs, inbox rules, CRM fields, templates, FAQs, examples, user permissions and approval rules.

3

Build and configure

XoomAI configures workflows, knowledge, guardrails, escalation paths, reporting and integrations.

4

Test and review

Run sample work, check outputs, adjust tone, verify routing, confirm permissions and refine edge cases.

5

Train the team

Show staff what the agent does, how to request work, how approvals work and when to escalate.

6

Launch and optimise

Go live with monitoring, reporting and continuous improvement as part of the managed service.
